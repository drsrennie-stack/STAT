Your Commitment

Before Week 1 opens, we ask you to read this commitment and sign it. This is not a legal contract. It's a structural commitment to the work the next 8 weeks will require, and an honest acknowledgment of what the program does and does not promise.

01

What this program is

STAT Success Cohort is an 8-week cohort-based recovery program for students currently failing or at serious risk of failing Anatomy and Physiology. It is built on the principle that failing students need structural intervention, not more information.

The program runs weekly live team-based sessions. Between sessions, you complete a weekly Current State audit, participate in a team case discussion, and commit to a personal decision plan. The rhythm is called the CODE cycle: Current state, Observe, Decide, Evaluate. That cycle is the product.

You will be assigned to a small team of 4 to 6 students with a trained facilitator. Your team works together every week. Your progress is connected to theirs.

02

What this program does not promise

⚠ Read this carefully

This program does not promise that you will pass your Anatomy and Physiology course. It does not promise a specific grade. It does not promise that you will qualify for your program. Your A&P grade is determined by your instructor, your institution, and your own execution, not by participation in this program.

What this program does commit to: providing the structure, methodology, team, and facilitation that give recovery its best chance. What happens with that infrastructure is in your hands.

Students who engage fully with the CODE cycle, show up to live sessions, do the weekly work, and communicate honestly with their team typically see meaningful recovery. Students who skim, skip, or treat the program as optional typically do not. This pattern is not unique to this program. It's the pattern of recovery work in any structured setting.

03

Your commitments

Four commitments. Read each one, and check it if you can genuinely commit to it. If you cannot commit to all four, this program is not the right fit right now, and there are other paths forward we can discuss with you.

A

Attendance

I will attend all 8 live weekly sessions, each approximately 90 minutes. If a genuine emergency prevents attendance, I will notify my facilitator in advance and watch the recording within 48 hours. I understand that missing more than one session significantly impacts my team and my own recovery, and that I am permitted one structured re-entry during the program.

E

Engagement

I will complete my weekly Current State audit before each session and my decision plan after each session. I will engage actively in the team case discussion during the Observe phase, not just observe. I will execute my weekly commitments to the best of my ability, and report honestly when I don't.

H

Honesty

I will report truthfully on my Current State audits and decision plan execution. Performance reporting, where I tell my team what I think they want to hear rather than what actually happened, makes recovery impossible. I understand that honest reporting of failures is more valuable than false reporting of successes.

T

Team

I will show up for my team as much as I ask them to show up for me. This means engaging with their work, not just mine. Contributing to case discussions. Respecting confidentiality within the team. Naming what I observe when it would help, and offering support when it's needed. A team is not a group of individuals sharing a Zoom link. It's a small accountable community.

04

What we commit to

In exchange, STAT Success Cohort commits to: trained facilitators who know the methodology and protect the space, weekly sessions that run on time and follow a consistent structure, clear tools for Current State audits and decision plans, cases that reflect real recovery situations, responsiveness to your questions between sessions, and an environment that takes your situation seriously without minimizing it or panicking about it.

We commit to treating you as a capable adult in a temporarily difficult situation, not as a broken student requiring rescue. The program works because you do the work. Our job is to make sure the structure around that work is sound.
