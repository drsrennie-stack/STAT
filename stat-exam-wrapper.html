STAT Success Cohort · Week 4 Optional Tool

Exam wrapper.

A structured reflection for after you get an exam back. Helps you see not just what you got wrong, but what kind of error it was and what to change. Use this for your midterm, or for any graded exam you want to learn from.

How to use this tool

Have your graded exam in front of you when you start. Work through the sections in order. Section 1, basic exam information. Section 2, how you prepared. Section 3, a breakdown of your wrong answers by error type. Section 4, patterns and what to change. Takes 15 to 20 minutes. Auto-saves as you go.

02

How you prepared

Be honest. This section is most useful when you describe what you actually did, not what you meant to do.

One day cramming scores very differently from 7 days of spaced work, even at the same hour total.

1 = almost entirely passive (rereading, watching videos). 5 = almost entirely active (retrieval, self-testing, explaining out loud).

1 = walking into a disaster. 5 = felt genuinely prepared.

List briefly. Textbook, lecture notes, practice questions, study group, office hours, YouTube, etc.

03

Breakdown of wrong answers

Go through your exam. For each question you got wrong, classify the error type. The total should equal the number of points you lost. If a single question is worth multiple points, you can split it across categories.

Did not study this content The material was in the syllabus, but you did not study it, or studied it superficially.
Studied but did not understand You covered it but it did not click. You could not have explained it to someone else.
Understood but could not apply You knew the concept but could not use it on this question. Application failure, not knowledge failure.
Foundational gap You needed a prerequisite concept you did not have (from A&P 1, chemistry, or earlier in this course).
Misread the question You knew the answer but misread what was asked. Reading errors, ambiguous wording, missed a "not" or "except."
Test anxiety / blanking You knew the material while studying but could not retrieve it during the exam.
Time pressure / careless You rushed and made errors you would not have made with more time. Arithmetic, switched answers, ran out of time.
Physical or visual recognition failure Lab practical only. You knew the structure by name and function but could not identify it on the model or slide.

04

What to change for next time

Based on the pattern above, commit to specific changes. These feed directly into your decision plan for this week.

Was there a question you were sure you got right but did not? A whole topic area you did not expect? A mismatch between your confidence and your actual performance?

Which of your prep strategies produced right answers? These are things to keep doing.

Which strategies led to wrong answers or did not prepare you for how the exam actually tested the material? These are things to change.

What was not part of your prep that should have been? Office hours, specific practice, working with a different kind of material, getting help on a specific concept.

Be specific. Not "study more." Something like "do the first round of practice questions 5 days before the exam, not 2."
