STAT Success Cohort · Week 5 Optional Tool

Point recovery inventory.

Recovery shows in leading indicators before it shows in grades. This tool helps you surface the leading indicators you have been generating but not counting. Takes 15 to 20 minutes. Your work auto-saves as you go.

How to use this tool

Section 1, write a brief baseline of where you were in Week 1 across five domains. Section 2, write where you are today across the same five domains. Section 3, the comparison renders automatically. Section 4, pick the three biggest leading indicators that have moved. Section 5, contrast that with your grade. The point of this tool is to make leading indicators visible before they show up in your grade.

01

Your Week 1 baseline

Cast your mind back to Week 1. Use your Week 1 audit if you saved it. For each domain, write a sentence or two about where you were. Be honest, not retrospectively dramatic. Real description, not a story.

Study session quality
Focus during sessions, length you can sustain, what you actually got out of an hour of study.
Recall and retention
Whether you can pull up information without looking, whether last week's material still feels available.
Sleep and physical anxiety
Hours of sleep before exams, physical symptoms before or during exams, how the body feels around schoolwork.
Engagement
Whether you attend, whether you ask questions, whether you talk to peers about course material.
Confidence and energy
Whether you feel hope or dread when you think about the course. Whether you have energy left for it.

04

Your top three leading indicators

Pick the three biggest gains from the comparison above. Write each as a specific observation, not a feeling. "My recall is sharper" is a feeling. "I can answer questions in lecture without notes for the first time all semester" is an observation. Observations are what you trust when the grade has not yet caught up.
