Week 2 theme
Stop the bleeding.

In critical care, the first thing you do with an unstable patient is stop whatever is actively making things worse. Not everything gets fixed in the first intervention. You stabilize first. The same is true for academic recovery. Morgan's case is about identifying what is actively making things worse and stopping it before anything else.

Meet Morgan

Morgan is a 22-year-old pre-nursing student at a community college. They are in their second semester of Anatomy and Physiology, having passed A&P 1 with a C last semester. The current semester started in September and it is now early October. Four weeks in, Morgan has taken two of the five unit exams.

Morgan's current grade in A&P 2 is 58%. The first exam was 56. The second was 38. Three more unit exams, three more lab practicals, and the cumulative final are still to come.

Morgan in their own words

Morgan's facilitator asked them to describe their study approach and how things are going. This is what Morgan wrote.

Morgan, week 4 of the semester

"I do not understand what is happening. I study harder than anyone I know. I put in at least 15 hours a week on A&P, sometimes more. My notes are beautiful. I color-code them by system. I reread the chapters before lectures and again before exams. I use Quizlet almost every day. I watch the YouTube videos my professor recommends, sometimes twice."

"But when I sit down for the exam, my mind goes blank. I stare at the questions and everything I studied is just gone. I leave the exam feeling like I did not know half of what was asked. Then I get the grade back and I was right."

"I am starting to wonder if I am just not smart enough for nursing. My parents do not know about the second exam score yet. I have been avoiding my professor's office hours because I feel like I should know this stuff by now."

Morgan's typical study week

Morgan spends roughly 15 to 18 hours per week on A&P outside of class. Here is what that time actually looks like.

What Morgan does
  • Reads the chapter with highlighter before lecture
  • Takes handwritten notes during lecture
  • Color-codes notes at home afterward, by body system
  • Rereads the chapter a second time, underlining new details
  • Builds Quizlet cards, uses Learn mode
  • Watches professor-recommended YouTube videos at 1.5x speed
  • Reviews all materials the night before each exam
What Morgan does not do
  • Take practice exams or practice questions
  • Self-test without notes in hand
  • Study with other students
  • Attend office hours
  • Work with models or practicals outside of lab time
  • Explain concepts out loud to themselves or anyone else
  • Space study across multiple days per topic
P

The teaching point of Morgan's case

Morgan is not lazy. Morgan is not unintelligent. Morgan is studying 15 to 18 hours a week on this course. The problem is not effort. The problem is that the methods Morgan is using feel productive but do not produce the kind of learning that transfers to an exam. This is the most common pattern in failing A&P students. It is almost certainly a pattern you will recognize in yourself, in part.

Before the live session

You do not need to solve Morgan's case before the session. The whole point of the Observe phase is that your team works through it together, with your facilitator guiding. What you do need to do is read the case carefully and think about it.

Come to the session with these three things in mind

  • Where do you see yourself in Morgan's story? Be specific. Which habits are yours? Which "does not do" items apply to you? Not all of them will. Some will.
  • What do you think is actually happening with Morgan? Come in with a hypothesis. You do not need to be right. Your team will work this out together.
  • What would you want Morgan to do first? Not the full plan. Just the very first change. Remember the theme: stop the bleeding.
i

What you will do in the session

Your team will work through three questions together, guided by your facilitator. What do we think is happening for Morgan. What should Morgan stop doing. What should Morgan start doing. You will reason through it as a team, not as individuals. After the session, you will write your own decision plan, which applies what the team surfaced to your own situation.
