This week's CODE.

Week 3 turns from method to content. You have a new study approach from Week 2. Now the question is what you actually understand, what you do not, and which of the gaps are blocking you right now.

Week 3 theme
Foundation repair, selective.

In critical care, foundation repair happens after stabilization. You do not rebuild everything at once. You fix what is actively preventing the patient from getting better. The same is true here. You cannot relearn all of A&P 1 or chemistry this week. What you can do is identify the specific gaps that are blocking your current coursework and close them fast. Selective, not comprehensive.

The shift this week

Week 2 was about how you study. Week 3 is about what you understand.

Many students who are failing A&P do not just have a method problem. They also have accumulated gaps in foundational concepts that later material depends on. Action potentials depend on understanding ion gradients. Renal function depends on understanding osmosis. Muscle contraction depends on understanding protein structure. When the foundation is missing, no amount of studying the current topic will hold.

The goal this week is not to relearn everything. It is to find the specific foundation gaps that are blocking the material in front of you and selectively repair them.

P

The work of Week 3 is triage, not repair

You cannot close every gap this week. You are not supposed to. The work of Week 3 is identifying which gaps matter most right now, and closing those first. A missed concept from A&P 1 that is not relevant to current material can stay missing for now. One that is actively blocking what you are studying this week cannot.

Your focus for the week

Identify one to two specific foundation concepts that are blocking current material. For each one, find a source (YouTube video, textbook chapter, tutoring session, office hours) and close the gap. That is the week's work.

Be specific. "I need to review A&P 1" is not a gap. "I do not understand how the sodium-potassium pump establishes the resting membrane potential" is a gap. Specificity is what makes foundation repair possible.
