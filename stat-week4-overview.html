This week's CODE.

Week 4 is the midpoint. Most of you just took your course midterm, or will this week. This is the first time the program's work meets the real data. What the data says determines what Week 5 becomes.

Week 4 theme
The midterm hinge.

In clinical work, the midpoint of a patient's treatment course is where you reassess. Is the intervention working? What is improving? What is not? Based on the answer, you continue, escalate, or pivot. Week 4 works the same way. The midterm exam or your current grades are data, not verdict. Your job this week is to read the data honestly and decide what stays, what changes, and what needs to be added.

Why Week 4 matters

You have been in the program for three full weeks. You changed how you study. You identified foundation gaps and did selective repair. You showed up to your team. You made decisions and evaluated them.

Now the data is coming in. Midterm exam. Current course grade. Your own sense of what is clicking and what is not. This is the first point in the program where you can actually see whether the work is working.

Some of you will see clear improvement. Some will see mixed results. A few may see no improvement, or even a drop. All three outcomes are data worth having. The point of Week 4 is not to celebrate or panic. It is to read the data carefully and decide what comes next.

Your facilitator will also open space for team members to share their own midterm data if they choose to. This is voluntary. No one is required to share specific numbers. But students who are willing to put their own data in the room often get more out of the session.

P

The work of Week 4 is honest reading

Midterm results tempt two reactions. If the numbers are up, the temptation is relief and relaxation: "I've got this now." If the numbers are not up, the temptation is despair and paralysis: "Nothing is working." Both reactions hide the actual data. The work of Week 4 is to resist both temptations and ask the harder questions. What specifically is working. What specifically is not. What does the data tell me to do differently.

Your focus for the week

Read your own data the same way you will read Alex's. Without judgment. Without performance. Without drama. Just: what happened, why, and what do I do now.

Your decision plan this week has three parts:

Protect. What is working that you are going to keep doing regardless of whether the numbers moved yet. Real change sometimes shows up in the second half, not the first. Do not stop what is working.

Pivot. What is not working and needs to change. If lab practical is still low and lecture exam is improving, you cannot use the same study method for both. Lab requires different work.

Add. What has not been part of your plan that the midterm showed you needed. Office hours. Peer study. A specific resource. One thing, not ten.

i

If your midterm was bad

A bad midterm is not the end of the program or your course. It is a data point. If you are in a position where even a perfect second half will not pass the course, your facilitator will help you think about the realistic options, which may include a strategic withdrawal. That is a serious conversation, not a shameful one. Reach out to your facilitator this week if this applies to you.
