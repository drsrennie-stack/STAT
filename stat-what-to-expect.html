The weekly rhythm

Every week of STAT Success Cohort follows the same four-phase rhythm. It is called the CODE cycle: Current state, Observe, Decide, Evaluate. Think of it as running a code on your own academic situation, week after week, with your team.

C Current state happens before each live session. You complete a written audit on yourself that documents how the week went, what you're currently working with, and what you're bringing into the team discussion. Think of it as a clinical note you are writing on your own recovery. It takes about 15 minutes.
O Observe happens during the live session. Your team works together on a case, a composite failing student whose situation parallels your own in some way. You observe what is happening, reason through what they should do, and watch your facilitator guide the discussion. This is the pedagogical core of the program.
D Decide happens right after the session. You write a personal action plan translating what the team surfaced into three specific commitments for the week ahead, plus one small thing you will do in the next 24 hours. About 10 to 15 minutes.
E Evaluate happens the following week. Your next Current state audit opens by asking what actually happened with last week's decisions. Did you do what you said you would do? Honest reporting is the point.

That's the loop. It runs every week from Week 2 onward. Week 1 is slightly different because the team is still forming and there's no case yet.

Your time commitment

Expect roughly 3 to 4 hours per week for STAT Success Cohort work itself: one 90-minute live session, about 30 minutes on your Current state audit, about 15 minutes on your action plan, and 15 to 30 minutes of case pre-reading. That is separate from time spent on your actual A&P course.

!

⚠ Read this before signing the commitment

If 3 to 4 hours a week on top of your course work feels impossible right now, this program is not the right fit right now. That's information worth having before you sign the commitment contract in the next lesson.

Your team

You'll be assigned to a small team of 4 to 6 students with a trained facilitator. Your team meets at the same day and time every week for 8 weeks. Your progress is connected to theirs, and theirs to yours.

Teams are not support groups. They're not friend circles. They're small accountable working communities where other students in recovery do the work alongside you. Confidentiality matters. Reliability matters. Showing up for the team matters as much as the team showing up for you.

Communication between sessions

Your facilitator is your primary point of contact between sessions. You can reach them by email for anything time-sensitive. Response time is 24 to 48 hours on weekdays.

If you need to miss a session, you notify your facilitator in advance and watch the recording within 48 hours. The program allows one structured re-entry during the 8 weeks.

What this program does not promise

!

Be clear about this

This program does not promise you will pass your A&P course. It does not promise a specific grade. It does not promise you will qualify for your degree program. Your A&P grade is determined by your instructor, your institution, and your own execution, not by participation in STAT Success Cohort.

What the program commits to: structure, methodology, team, facilitation, and the best possible conditions for recovery work. What happens inside those conditions is in your hands.
