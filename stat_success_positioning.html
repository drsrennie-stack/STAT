STAT Success, Platform Positioning v1.0, April 2026

When your grade can't wait.

STAT Success is a clinical method for students in academic emergencies. Built for the student halfway through a semester with a failing grade and no time left for patience. Every component teaches students to triage, stabilize, treat, and monitor their own academic situation the way a clinician would.

01 Philosophy

Most study tools are built for stable students.

But stable students are not the ones seeking help. Students seek help when they are already struggling, already behind, already running out of time. STAT Success was built for the students the rest of the industry ignored.

The core insight

In clinical medicine, you don't always have time to make the perfect decision. You have time to triage, stabilize, and act on incomplete information before the patient deteriorates. STAT Success teaches students to apply that same logic to their own academic situation.

The student mid-semester with a 62% on their midterm doesn't need a twelve-week study plan. They need to identify the two or three topics that will appear on their next exam, deploy the right intervention protocol, and measure whether it's working, all in days, not weeks. That's academic triage. It's a different skill than long-term mastery, and almost no one is teaching it.

STAT Success is that missing thing. It's not a course. It's not a tutor. It's a clinical system for academic emergencies, with the student as the clinician working their own case.

02 The Three Acuities

Not every academic situation is a crisis.

The first job of STAT Success is to help a student diagnose the acuity of their own situation. Different acuities call for different strategies. This framework is the organizing principle of the entire platform.

Level 01
Stable
Grade in the B range, more than four weeks until next exam, confidence across most topics. Strategy Long-term mastery, deep learning, deliberate practice across topics. Tools Standard study resources. STAT Success is not required.
Level 02
Urgent
Grade in the C range, two to four weeks until next exam, low confidence in specific topics. Strategy Targeted intervention on diagnosed weak areas. Protocol-driven practice. Tools Grade Auditor triage, priority protocols, daily Vital Signs tracking.
Level 03
Critical
Grade at or below passing, less than two weeks until next exam, widespread uncertainty. Strategy Stabilize first, treat highest-yield topics only. Accept partial coverage. Tools 48-hour protocols, cognitive load reduction, Grand Rounds case support.
03 The Method

Four clinical phases, applied to your own learning.

Every rotation in STAT Success, regardless of subject, follows the same four phases. Students learn the method once in their first rotation and reuse it in every subsequent course. The framework is portable. The content changes.

i
Student runs

Triage

Student uses the Grade Auditor to diagnose acuity, identify the two or three topics that are bleeding points, and rank by urgency. Output: a personal acuity score and ranked treatment priorities.

ii
Student applies

Stabilize

Immediate interventions for the highest-priority topics. Active recall protocols, spaced review schedules, stop-the-bleed techniques for the nearest exam. Output: a stabilization plan for the next seven days.

iii
Student practices

Treat

Deep intervention on the diagnosed weak topics using subject-specific tools, teach-back protocols, and, where applicable, Grand Rounds team case work. Output: measurable mastery on the target topics.

iv
Student tracks

Monitor

Weekly self-assessment, grade trajectory tracking, early warning signs of deterioration, adjustment protocols. Output: sustained improvement into the next exam and beyond.

05 Student Profile

Built for the student no one else is building for.

STAT Success has a very specific customer. Everything, from the tagline to the tool design, is engineered for this student. Trying to serve someone outside this profile weakens the product.

Who STAT Success is for

  • A student currently enrolled in a prerequisite science or nursing course
  • Mid-semester, not at the start or end
  • Grade is below their goal, typically C range or lower
  • An exam is coming up in days to weeks, not months
  • Motivated, but running out of time and confidence
  • Willing to do the work, not looking for shortcuts or answers
  • Paying for themselves, or backed by a parent or institution who will

Who STAT Success is not for

  • Students looking for long-term mastery programs
  • Students who want content dumps without structure
  • Students who want someone else to do the thinking for them
  • Students still in the first quarter of a course
  • Students already earning A's who want enrichment
  • Students in high school or early college general education
  • Students looking for answer keys, cheat sheets, or test banks
06 Competitive Positioning

Not a gym. An urgent care.

STAT Success competes in a different category than most study tools, which changes the economics. Mass-market study tools are priced like gyms: cheap, subscription-based, optimized for general improvement. STAT Success is priced like urgent care: higher upfront cost, specific outcome, time-limited engagement.

The pricing reflects the stakes. A nursing student who fails A&P loses a year of progress and tens of thousands in deferred income. A premed student loses GPA competitiveness and possibly their application. Saving those outcomes is not a $20 purchase. STAT Success prices to the value of the rescue, which is substantial.

07 Pricing Model

Priced like intervention, not subscription.

Each rotation is sold as a time-limited intervention package. Students buy the rotation, follow the protocols, and graduate out of the product within weeks. The goal is not lifetime subscription revenue. The goal is effective rescue.

Anchor pricing, A&P STAT, direct to student: $297 for the full eight-week rotation access, including Grade Auditor, all protocols and tools, Grand Rounds participation, and a 30-day money-back guarantee tied to effort milestones. Tiered discounts for early enrollment and student hardship cases.
Licensing to institutions: Per-student license at $75 to $150 per enrolled student per semester, or flat institutional license at $3,000 to $15,000 per term depending on program size. Institution provides access as a supplemental resource to students enrolled in their own prerequisite or nursing courses.
Pilot cohort, summer 2026: First 20 students at founding pricing ($197) in exchange for detailed feedback and permission to use their testimonials and outcome data in future marketing.
